For given $m \times n$ matrix A, we can always find orthogonal matrices
U $(m \times m)$ and V $(n \times n)$ such that $$U^T A V = \Sigma 
= \left ( 
\begin{array}{ccccc} 
  \sigma_1 & \ldots & & & \\
  \vdots  & \sigma_2 & \ldots & & \\
  &  & \ddots & & \\
  & & & \sigma_p 
\end{array} 
\right )$$ where p=min(m,n). The values $ \sigma_i $ are called the
singular values of $A$.
